Baxter: Constrained motion



Summary

The objective of this project was to enable baxter to find a cup on the table using camera vision and IR Sensor, then handle the cup to the right hand of the person in front of him without tipping the cup. The right hand was sensed by applying skeleton tracking on pointcloud data from a depth camera, mounted on the head of the baxter.



Step 1: Find the cup

The first step is to figure out where the cup is. Baxter is looking for a red cup in the experiment.

● Baxter will start from the home position , which is in such a way that the camera on the limb is horizontal to the table in front of it.

● Baxter will start sweeping the table looking for a red cup by using the ROS node "scan". At the same the ROS node "track_cup" will try to locate the centre of the red cup. OpenCV is used for this.

● Once the centre of the camera view is same as the centre of the cup, the "move_to_cup" node is started. This node moves the arm from current position towards the cup until it is close enough to grasp the cup, and closes the gripper. The distance from cup to the gripper is found using the IR sensor below the gripper



Step 2: Skeleton tracking

● Once the gripper is closed the skeleton tracking starts which is done by using the "skeletontracker_nu" package from northwestern NxR lab. The package filters the output from the Asus Xtion depth camera, to identify people in its view, who are represented as skeletons. The skeleton is a collection of points representing different joints.

● The "filter_V2" node select the closest skeleton in front of the camera. Then locates the 3D point corresponding to the right hand of the skeleton.



Step 3: Follow the right hand.

In this step the baxter have to follow the right hand of the person with the cup without tipping it over. MoveIt package from ROS was used for this purpose. We used different approaches to find the optimum one

● Our first approach was to find the goal pose to MoveIt, and plan the path in cartesian space with an orientation constraint. This worked but used a lot of time for calculations.

● Final approach was to find a joint space solution for the goal pose and input the joint positions to MoveIt, to plan a path in joint space.

● If we remove the orientation constraint the response time was better, and as the start and end pose are same, even if the orientation constraint is removed the cup was never tipped over in most case
